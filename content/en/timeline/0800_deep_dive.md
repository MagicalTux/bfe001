I remember taking the RER across the Paris banlieue to a library, where I
photocopied an entire book about Amiga internals. I wanted to know what
every function in every library did. I discovered you could hook library
functions by overwriting the jump table offset to your own
address—because the Amiga shared one flat address space across all
tasks, with no memory protection. I upgraded from Workbench 1.3 to
2.04, expanded storage with an Iomega Zip drive (which promptly lost most
of my work to the infamous click of death).
