Week of March 16–22. 634 commits — the biggest week of the year so
far. The two PHP reimplementations took 556 of those between them.

## goro ↔ goro-rs

**[goro](https://github.com/KarpelesLab/goro)** took 281 commits and
**[goro-rs](https://github.com/KarpelesLab/goro-rs)** took 275 — the
Rust port, only a week old, has already caught up to the Go side in
commit velocity. The pattern I mentioned last week is paying off:
every PHP test that fails on one runtime gets looked at on the
other, and corner cases (parser edge cases, internal-function
semantics, error-message text) converge faster than either runtime
would on its own.

## mpcegosign

**[mpcegosign](https://github.com/KarpelesLab/mpcegosign)** — 13
commits on a new project. EGo's `ego sign` normally relies on a
single RSA-3072 private key to sign SGX enclaves; `mpcegosign`
replaces that with **t-of-n threshold RSA** — any t of n parties can
sign, no single party can sign alone. Keygen is interactive through
any group-chat medium (shares are X25519 ECDH + AES-256-GCM, so
pasting them into Slack / Signal / email is safe), MRENCLAVE
computation stays compatible with `ego-oesign`. Lets an org spread
the signing key across multiple people or devices without changing
how enclaves are consumed downstream.
