Week of March 9–15. 439 commits — a big one — spread across a lot of
threads.

## goro-rs: PHP in Rust, in parallel with PHP in Go

**[goro](https://github.com/KarpelesLab/goro)** took 187 commits and
the new **[goro-rs](https://github.com/KarpelesLab/goro-rs)**
(seeded the 14th) took 75 on its first two days. Same goal, two
target languages: a PHP 8.5 implementation that runs the PHP test
suite. Keeping both runtimes moving in lockstep against the same
reference tests is a structural forcing function — you end up with
two independent readings of every ambiguous spec corner, and each
one catches bugs the other would have missed.

## portablesql splits into drivers

**[portablesql/psql](https://github.com/portablesql/psql)** took 23
more commits, but the bigger shape change: the driver layer moved
into dedicated repos —
[psql-sqlite](https://github.com/portablesql/psql-sqlite),
[psql-pgsql](https://github.com/portablesql/psql-pgsql),
[psql-mysql](https://github.com/portablesql/psql-mysql),
and a shared [psql-test](https://github.com/portablesql/psql-test)
integration-test harness that runs the core against every driver
together. The core stays lean; drivers can ship on their own
cadence.

## bfe001.net

Quieter milestone: **[bfe001](https://github.com/MagicalTux/bfe001)**
— the repo behind this site — got its first commits on the 13th.
45 of them by the end of the week setting up the Amiga hardware
section, memory-map layout, multi-language plumbing, and the
initial timeline. It'll be a while before the site is worth pointing
at, but the bones are in.
